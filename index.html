A Tribute to Blackadder

The beloved Blackadder characters throughout the ages

The Four Series

There are four series of Blackadder, each set in a different historical period. There were also some Christmas specials, but the less said about them, the better.

Series 1: The Black Adder

Historical setting

Set in 1485 during the reign of (the fictional) Richard IV.

Key characters

  • Prince Edmund, "The Black Adder" (Rowan Atkinson)
  • Baldrick (Tony Robinson)
  • Lord Percy Percy (Tim McInnerny)
  • King Richard IV (Brian Blessed)

Every time I watch this series, I am pleasantly surprised to find that it isn't as bad as I remember it being. It's still not a patch on the later three series, but it does have some good moments. Baldrick is perhaps the smartest character of them all, trying his best to save his dimwitted boss from the errors of his foolhardy schemes to seize the crown from his father and brother. Brian Blessed is, of course, an utter delight.

Series 2: Blackadder II

Historical setting

Set during the reign of Elizabeth I.

Key characters

  • Edmund, Lord Blackadder (Rowan Atkinson)
  • Baldrick (Tony Robinson)
  • Lord Percy Percy (Tim McInnerny)
  • Queenie (Miranda Richardson)
  • Lord Melchett (Stephen Fry)
  • Nursie (Patsy Byrne)

I think this is my favourite of all the series, though it's a close run thing between this and series 4. The character of Edmund is established as cunning, witty and shrewd, as he tries to maintain his position in court while vying with his rival Melchett for the Queen's favour, treading carefully to ensure the whims of the terrifyingly fickle Queenie don't result in his death warrant.

Series 3: Blackadder the Third

Historical setting

Set in the late 18th century during the Regency period.

Key characters

  • Edmund Blackadder esquire (Rowan Atkinson)
  • Baldrick (Tony Robinson)
  • Prince George the Prince Regent (Hugh Laurie)
  • Mrs Miggins (Helen Atkinson-Wood)

Hugh Laurie is a brilliant addition to the regular cast in this series. Another idiot that the intelligent but acerbic Edmund has to deal with, he has some wonderful comic moments and is blissfully unaware of how his manservant manipulates him to his own advantage. There are some excellent guest cast appearances from regulars in other series too, including Stephen Fry as the Duke of Wellington, and Miranda Richardson as a squirrel-hating highwayman.

Series 4: Blackadder Goes Forth

Historical setting

Set during the First World War.

Key characters

  • Edmund Blackadder (Rowan Atkinson)
  • Baldrick (Tony Robinson)
  • Lieutenant George (Hugh Laurie)
  • General Melchett (Stephen Fry)
  • Captain Darling (Tim McInnerny)

This series amazingly manages to both give an insight into how truly terrible the war was, and also be incredibly funny. The ingenuity (and increasing ridiculousness) of Edmund's plans to escape the trenches are a joy to behold. The poignancy of the final scene of the final episode, when they finally "go over the top", is a true moment of TV history.
